Problems by vertical

What each practice type is actually struggling with, and how to position RepeatMD against the specific shape of their pain. Cards covered: med spa, cosmetic dermatology, plastic surgery, health & wellness.

Gap flag: Med spas are the flagship vertical, but the source Needs/Objections doc doesn’t have a dedicated med-spa section. This sketch is drafted from the product decks and the ICP doc. Mike to validate or rewrite from lived experience.

Who they are

Multi-provider med spas with four or more cash-pay service categories. Established (four-plus years), 100-plus Google reviews at 4.5 stars or higher. Often already running some version of a membership or loyalty program that isn’t producing the results they expected. The highest-resonance fit for the platform.

What lands: the cross-sell problem, the after-hours problem, the retention drop-off problem. They already understand recurring revenue mechanics. The pitch is an upgrade, not an education.

What they say on calls

  • “Patients come for Botox but don’t try our other services.”
  • “We have a loyalty program but it’s manual and clunky.”
  • “We run promotions but can’t track the ROI.”
  • “Patients drop off after the first few visits.”
  • “Our front desk is buried in booking inquiries from after hours.”

Four core pain points

01
The cross-sell problem

Patients come for one service and don’t discover the rest of the menu. The front desk doesn’t have time to educate. The result is a single-treatment relationship instead of a multi-treatment one.Position: in-app rewards and AI advisors surface the full menu without the front desk doing extra work.

02
The retention drop-off

First-time patients don’t come back the way regulars do. Memberships exist but are not driving repeat visits at the rate they should. The economics break because acquisition cost is amortized across too few visits.Position: automated retention, rewards stacking, and member-only pricing convert first-timers into repeat patients.

03
The promo ROI blind spot

They run promotions because everyone runs promotions, but they can’t see which ones worked, for whom, or why. They keep doing the same campaigns hoping for better results.Position: centralized reporting and sales analytics show exactly which offer drove which sale, by patient.

04
The after-hours leakage

Patients are shopping at 10pm. They want to book, buy a gift card, buy a package, ask a question. The front desk is closed. The competitor down the street has an app.Position: Med-Commerce engine plus AI Treatment Advisors capture demand that’s otherwise lost overnight.

One-line hooks for openers

  • If your patients are coming back for Botox but never trying the rest of your menu, that’s the cross-sell problem RepeatMD was built to solve.
  • You don’t need more first-time patients. You need the ones you have to come back four more times.
  • Most of your patients are shopping when you’re closed. The question is whether they’re shopping you or shopping someone else.
  • Loyalty programs work. Manual loyalty programs don’t. We’re the difference between the two.

Who they are

Fragmented vertical. Cosmetic-first practices look more like med spas. Medical-first practices with a cosmetic add-on are a different conversation entirely (and often not a fit). The ones we sell are doctor-led practices with three or more cosmetic services and a real cash-pay revenue line.

They see themselves above the med spa register. The framing has to be premium. They want EMR integration with NextTech or Modernizing Medicine. 90 percent want memberships or retention automation. They don’t want to be sold a “discounting” tool.

What they say on calls

  • “I’m a doctor, not a med spa.”
  • “Does this integrate with NextTech?”
  • “We don’t discount.”
  • “The app looks generic, not custom enough to our brand.”
  • “What can’t we list because of insurance and legal?”

Four core pain points

01
System fragmentation

EMR for clinical, a different system for cosmetic, a third for marketing, a fourth for retention. Nothing talks to anything. The admin burden is real and growing.Position: RepeatMD is the unified patient-facing layer. It complements the EMR, doesn’t replace it.

02
The brand register problem

They won’t use a tool that makes their practice look like a Groupon med spa. Generic discounting language is a non-starter. Membership and premium experience are the right doors.Position: lead with the “Sephora-style app for your practice” framing. Memberships, not discounts. Premium, not promotional.

03
Cosmetic revenue underdevelopment

The cosmetic side of a derm practice often runs at a fraction of its real revenue potential because the clinical side absorbs the operational attention. They know it. They want to fix it without hiring a full cosmetic team.Position: automate cross-sell, after-hours capture, and retention so the cosmetic side scales without proportional staff growth.

04
Practice valuation

Owners thinking about exit, succession, or rolling up know that recurring revenue and digital infrastructure increase practice valuation. Memberships and a branded app are concrete moves toward a higher exit multiple.Position: recurring memberships can increase practice valuation 3 to 5x. The branded app is a durable asset.

One-line hooks for openers

  • RepeatMD isn’t a med spa tool, it’s the layer that lets your cosmetic side scale without growing your staff.
  • Your EMR runs the back office. RepeatMD runs the cosmetic-revenue line.
  • Memberships don’t mean discounts. They mean recurring revenue and a higher exit multiple.
  • If your patients can’t buy from you at 10pm, your cosmetic ceiling is already in.

Who they are

Surgical-led practices that have added or want to add a non-surgical menu. Big-ticket procedures, low-volume relationships. They consider themselves above med spas and don’t want to use the same tools. The fit conversation is about expanding into aesthetics, not optimizing what they already do.

The decision-maker is usually the surgeon. The champion is usually the practice manager or marketing director. The Patient Care Coordinator runs initial consults and is effectively the practice’s sales rep.

What they say on calls

  • “Once surgery is done, we don’t need to see the patient again.”
  • “We don’t want pricing transparency, our procedures are quoted in consultation.”
  • “Patients already have Allē or Aspire loyalty, why a competing program?”
  • “Cherry gives us 2.9 percent at 24 months at zero.”
  • “Will this disrupt our consultation workflow?”

Four core pain points

01
No relationship after surgery

The surgical patient is a one-time relationship by design. The practice spent meaningful CAC to win a single transaction and then loses the patient forever. The non-surgical menu is the answer; the infrastructure to support it isn’t there.Position: convert the one-time surgical patient into a recurring non-surgical patient. Memberships, packages, rewards across the non-surgical menu.

02
Cross-sell into non-surgical is broken

They have a non-surgical menu but their patients don’t know about it. The surgical workflow doesn’t introduce the non-surgical options. The cross-sell never happens.Position: AI Treatment Advisors and in-app rewards introduce the non-surgical menu without the surgeon having to recommend it directly.

03
Loyalty program competition

Allergan’s Allē and Galderma’s Aspire already have their patients enrolled. The practice doesn’t want to compete with the manufacturer programs that drive their injectable volume.Position: RepeatMD is brand-agnostic and stacks alongside Allē/Aspire. It rewards practice-level loyalty across the full menu, not manufacturer-specific volume.

04
Financing economics

Cherry, CareCredit, PatientFi all have specific plans plastics use heavily. The financing conversation is concrete and comparative. Affirm at 3.99 percent flat with 81 percent approval is a real differentiator, but only if positioned against what they’re already using.Position: head-to-head financing comparison. Flat practice fee, soft credit check, higher approvals, no late fees to patients, $22,500 line.

One-line hooks for openers

  • The surgical patient is a one-time transaction. The non-surgical patient is a lifetime relationship. RepeatMD is how you convert one into the other.
  • RepeatMD doesn’t compete with Allē, it stacks on top of it.
  • Your patients spend $20,000 on surgery and never come back. We give them a reason to.
  • If your financing fee is 8 percent and approval is 60 percent, we should talk.

Who they are

Broad category. IV therapy clinics, integrative medicine practices, weight-loss focused wellness centers, longevity clinics. The fit varies enormously. Standalone IV bars look different from full wellness practices with semaglutide, peptides, and aesthetic add-ons.

Most are newer (one to five years), founder-as-provider, and in growth mode. The shift toward GLP-1s, longevity, and peptides is the big trend, and weight-loss packages plus financing is a proven combination. Many have aspirations to sell retail products but lack the infrastructure.

What they say on calls

  • “We just added semaglutide and we need to build volume.”
  • “Standalone IV memberships have been hard to get patients to commit to.”
  • “Our patients ask for products we don’t stock.”
  • “We’re using Vagaro / GoHighLevel / Square, does this overlap?”
  • “Our patient base is new, will rewards even matter yet?”

Four core pain points

01
Building volume on new high-margin services

GLP-1s, peptides, longevity panels. High margin, high demand, but the practice is young and needs to convert curiosity into committed patients fast. The structure isn’t there yet.Position: package these as 3, 6, 12-month commitments with financing built in. Use AI advisors to convert after-hours questions into bookings.

02
Membership friction

Wellness patients are interested in memberships but the practice can’t operationalize them. Manual enrollment, manual renewal, no in-app discovery. The recurring-revenue lever is sitting unused.Position: one-click membership sales, automated renewal, member-only pricing, in-app member benefits.

03
Retail aspiration, no infrastructure

They want to sell supplements, skincare, wearables. They don’t have inventory capital or shipping operations. They lose the retail revenue to Amazon and direct-to-consumer brands.Position: SkinDrop solves inventory entirely. 3,000-plus products, 30 percent margin, zero upfront cash, RepeatMD handles fulfillment.

04
Tool overlap anxiety

Wellness practices have often patched together five tools that almost do what they want. They’re reasonably worried about adding a sixth that overlaps with the five.Position: be specific about what RepeatMD does and doesn’t replace. The patient-facing experience, the rewards engine, the financing layer. Not a replacement for booking or EMR.

One-line hooks for openers

  • If you just added semaglutide, the question isn’t whether to package and finance it. It’s how fast.
  • Your patients are buying their supplements somewhere. Today it’s Amazon. We’ll make it you.
  • Memberships are the difference between a wellness practice and a wellness business.
  • We’re not replacing your booking tool. We’re the layer your patients actually want to use.
